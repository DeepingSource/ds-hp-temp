A guide to what the colors in a store heatmap mean and how to use them for shelf and display layout. You can see the actual screen in the heatmap analysis section of the store insight user manual.

## What Is a Heatmap

A heatmap is a report that visualizes, through color intensity, where in the store customer movement and dwell are concentrated. Its distinguishing feature is that you can grasp the flow at a glance on the store floor plan, rather than from a table of numbers.

## How to Read the Colors

- **Dark colors (hot areas)** — places where customer movement and dwell are heavily concentrated.
- **Light colors (cold areas)** — places where relatively few customers pass through or stay.
- Rather than the absolute intensity of the color itself, it is more useful to compare the **relative differences between zones within the same store**.

## Comparing by Time of Day

By dividing the day into time periods such as morning, lunch, and evening and comparing heatmaps, you can see which zones get busy at which times of day. For example, the area around the checkout may appear dark at lunchtime, while a particular shelf may appear dark in the evening. When you find such differences, you can use them as a basis for varying staff allocation or promotion placement by time of day.

## How to Use It

- Placing new products or high-margin items in dark areas can increase their exposure.
- For light areas, examine whether there is room to guide customers by changing the customer flow or the display.
- Viewing it alongside other reports such as zone-level interest and purchase conversion rate lets you distinguish between "many people come" and "they actually show interest."

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between a heatmap and zone-level analysis?
Zone-level analysis is a report that compares numbers by pre-configured zones, while a heatmap visually shows the flow across the entire store without zone divisions. Viewing them together is more accurate.

### Can I view a heatmap for only a specific date?
Yes. You can specify the desired date or period using the period selector at the top.

### The store floor plan looks different from the actual store.
The floor plan, lines, or zone settings may differ from the actual store. Check them in the dashboard settings or contact your account manager.
