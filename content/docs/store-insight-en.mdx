store insight is a spatial analytics dashboard that uses data to reveal the customer journey that flows from **visit → browse → consider → purchase** across your store. This guide walks you through everything you need for day-to-day operations, from logging in to interpreting reports.

[View the official user guide (v3.5)](https://guide.storeinsight.ai/v35/)

## What is store insight

Because it analyzes CCTV footage after anonymization, you can understand what happens in your store space without identifying any individual. store insight supports store decision-making in four ways.

- **Operational efficiency** — Position staff and displays based on the zones where people cluster.
- **Marketing performance** — Design campaigns tailored to high-interest zones and visitor characteristics.
- **Customer experience** — Use customer-flow and dwell data to find layouts that reduce shopping fatigue.
- **Data-driven decisions** — Decide quickly based on evidence, not gut feeling.

## Getting started

### Logging in

You access store insight through a web browser. The access URL and account are issued by your administrator for each store.

1. Go to the access URL provided by your administrator.
2. Enter your ID and password.
3. Click the login button to enter the main dashboard.

![store insight login screen](/images/si-guide/01-login.png)
*Login screen — sign in with the ID and password issued by your administrator*

### Exploring the dashboard

After logging in, you see a store overview summary and can select a report menu on the left (Store Overview · Space Value · Customer Journey · Diagnostics & Optimization · Custom Reports). Each report is displayed as cards, charts, or heatmaps.

### Period selection and filters

Use the period selector (date picker) at the top to set the analysis period, and use filters to narrow conditions such as day of week and time of day. Most reports are recalculated instantly based on the selected period and filters. If data appears empty, check the period and filters first.

## Dashboard setup

### Setting up lines and zones

On your store floor plan, set up the **lines** (virtual lines that count passing traffic) and **zones** (analysis areas such as shelves and corners) that serve as the basis for analysis. Placing a line at the entrance lets you measure the entry rate, and placing a zone at each shelf lets you measure interest by zone.

### Registering event schedules

By registering promotion and event schedules in advance, you can compare changes in visits and conversion during those periods, making it easy to verify event effectiveness afterward.

## Glossary

- **Passerby** — Someone who passed in front of the store but did not enter.
- **Visitor** — A customer who actually entered the store.
- **Entry rate** — The ratio of visitors to passersby — a metric that gauges the appeal of the entrance and exterior.
- **Dwell time** — The time a visitor stayed in the store or a specific zone.
- **Line** — A virtual line set on the screen to measure passing traffic.
- **Zone** — An area within the store set as a unit of analysis (shelves, corners, etc.).
- **Customer flow** — The path a visitor moved along within the store.
- **Zone interest** — An efficiency metric comparing actual approach and dwell against zone exposure.
- **Funnel** — The stage-by-stage conversion flow from visit to purchase.
- **Purchase conversion rate** — The ratio of buyers to visitors.

## Frequently asked questions

### If you can't log in
Check that your ID and password are correct and that Caps Lock is not on. If it still doesn't work, clear your browser cache and try again, and contact your administrator regarding your account.

### I don't know the access URL
The access URL and account are issued by your administrator for each store. Please contact your administrator.

### I can't see any data
Check the period selection and filter conditions at the top. If no data was collected during the selected period, it may appear empty.
