Looking at the recommended quantity alone makes it hard to judge "why this number." Order Rationale Tracing explains how the recommended order quantity was derived by tracing it back to sales flow and inventory criteria. It lets a manager check the calculation background before accepting the quantity as is.

## Key Features

- **Decision variables** — Presents the 60-day average sales, forecast sales, recent trend, and safety stock criteria as the basis.
- **Trend classification** — Groups products into rising, stable, and declining to show the background of the quantity calculation.
- **ABC grade** — Classifies each product's importance into grades and reflects it in order decisions.
- **Waste risk and order history** — Points out products with high waste risk along with recent order records.

## How to Use

1. First, review the order recommendation results.
2. Request the order rationale for a recommendation whose basis you want to understand.
3. Check the calculation process in the key summary and detail list.
4. Adjust the order quantity if needed.

*Key summary and detail list. The calculation basis—the number of target products, the final total, and the value rounded up after multiplying by the safety stock multiplier—is organized item by item.*

## Tips

Even for a product with a declining trend, the quantity may be maintained if there is concern about lost sales opportunities. Deciding the final order after checking the basis in the detail items can help reduce both stockouts and overstocking.
