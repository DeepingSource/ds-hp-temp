A guide to interpreting the two metrics you will see most often on the dashboard—dwell time and purchase conversion rate—both individually and together.

## Dwell Time

Dwell time is the amount of time a visitor spends in the store or in a particular zone. However, **longer is not always better.** Its meaning varies by business type—for a business such as a cafe, where staying is itself the purpose, a longer dwell time can be a positive signal, but for a business such as a convenience store, where quick purchase is the goal, an excessively long dwell time may be a signal that the customer is "wandering, unable to find the product they are looking for."

## Purchase Conversion Rate

The purchase conversion rate is the proportion of visitors that led to an actual purchase. It is a key metric that shows the efficiency of the store as a whole, and it can only be viewed when POS is integrated. Looking at it by zone lets you pinpoint which shelf is a "they look but don't buy" segment.

## Interpreting Them in Combination

Rather than viewing the two metrics separately, viewing them together lets you narrow down the cause more precisely.

- **Long dwell + low conversion** — a state where customers examine thoroughly but it does not lead to a purchase—it is worth checking price, product assortment, and stock.
- **Short dwell + high conversion** — a state where purposeful purchases are made quickly—the customer flow and display are likely efficient.
- **Short dwell + low conversion** — a state where customers pass by without their interest being drawn at all—first check the display position or exposure.
- **Long dwell + high conversion** — a generally ideal state where customers browse thoroughly and purchase with satisfaction.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can the purchase conversion rate be viewed without POS integration?
No. The purchase conversion rate requires sales data, so it can only be viewed when POS is integrated.

### Can I compare against the average value for my business type?
We recommend using the metrics first and foremost to compare a store's own changes over different periods. If you need an industry benchmark, contact your account manager.

### The dwell time is exceptionally long in only one specific zone—is that a good signal?
It depends on the business type and the nature of that zone. We recommend checking the purchase conversion rate of the same zone together to make a judgment.
