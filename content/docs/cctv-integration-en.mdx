This guide explains how to connect your existing CCTV cameras to the DeepingSource anonymization analytics pipeline. The exact configuration for each site is confirmed together during the pre-inspection step of the onboarding process.

## Integration Principles

CCTV integration is **not about installing new cameras, but about connecting the video streams of your existing cameras to the analytics pipeline**. Video from the cameras is anonymized in real time before it is stored, and the original footage itself does not remain in the DeepingSource system.

## Integration Flow

The path from video to report goes through the following flow.

1. Video streams are pulled from the store's CCTV (or NVR/DVR).
2. AI anonymizes the people in the video in real time — personally identifiable information such as faces is removed, and only behavioral attributes such as movement and dwell remain.
3. The anonymized data is transmitted to and stored on the analytics server. The original footage is not retained during this process.
4. Based on the accumulated data, reports are generated in the dashboard.

## Integration Methods

- Most stores integrate by using the **video collected on the NVR/DVR** or the **stream from IP cameras** (common network camera streaming methods such as RTSP) as-is.
- The equipment configuration required for integration may vary depending on the camera and NVR manufacturer and the store's network structure, so it is confirmed individually during the pre-inspection.
- Integration equipment is installed to a minimum, and as a rule the process proceeds without building a separate large-scale server.

## Information to Prepare

Integration goes faster if your point of contact organizes the following information in advance.

- **Camera and NVR list** — A list organizing the manufacturer and model, installation location, and quantity.
- **Access credentials** — NVR/DVR administrator account or account information that can access the camera streams.
- **Network diagram** — A simple diagram or description of how the cameras, NVR, and internet line are connected.
- **On-site contact** — A contact who can open the door or guide equipment placement on-site on the day of installation.

## Verifying the Integration

Once integration is complete, your account manager checks whether each camera's stream is being received properly. After data begins to accumulate, you can view it as reports in the dashboard — see the Getting Your First Report document for details.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can old analog CCTV also be integrated?
If it can output video over the network through an NVR/DVR, integration is often possible. The exact feasibility is confirmed during the pre-inspection.

### Does CCTV recording stop during the integration work?
As a rule, integration is set up to operate separately from your existing recording system. Store-specific exceptions are explained during the pre-inspection.

### Do I need to integrate again if I replace a camera?
Yes, if you replace a camera or NVR, you may need to recheck the integration settings. If a replacement is planned, please let us know in advance.
