A guide to the order and mindset in which you should read multiple reports so you can avoid hasty conclusions.

## Status → Space → Journey → Diagnosis

With so many reports, it can be daunting to know where to start. Following the structure of the store insight manual gives you a natural order.

1. **Store status** — start with the big picture of how many people come and who they are right now.
2. **Spatial value** — use heatmaps and zone-level analysis to see which spaces are responding.
3. **Customer journey** — follow the customer flow to see how people move.
4. **Diagnosis and optimization** — use zone interest, funnel, and conversion rate to pinpoint where the leaks are.

## Do Not Jump to Conclusions From a Single Number

- Rather than a single day's number, viewing the **flow over several days or weeks** is more stable. There may be temporary factors such as a particular day of the week, weather, or an event.
- Rather than judging "good/bad" from a single metric alone, you should view **related metrics together**, such as dwell and conversion, to get closer to the cause.
- Rather than a simple comparison between stores, we recommend first looking at **the changes of the same store over time**. Because each store differs in location, floor area, and business type, comparing absolute values can be distorted.

## Every Time You View a Report

- First, check that the period and filters are set to the conditions you want.
- If there is a number that looks odd, recall whether there were any special circumstances during the same period, such as an event or a closure.
- Before drawing a conclusion, check the other related reports (heatmap, conversion rate, etc.) one more time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to check every day?
Not necessarily. Checking the flow on a weekly or monthly basis is also plenty useful.

### I want to compare with another store.
If the location, floor area, or business type differs, comparing absolute values can be distorted. We recommend comparing by each store's rate of change relative to a period.

### A specific metric changed drastically all of a sudden.
First check the period and filter settings, and if it still looks odd, capture the screen and contact your account manager.
