A guide to reading change by comparing periods—such as month-over-month or year-over-year—and what to watch for when comparing alongside event periods.

## Change Over Absolute Values

The number "1,000 visitors this month" alone makes it hard to judge whether you are doing well. It only becomes meaningful when you see whether it increased or decreased compared with last month, or with the same month last year. As covered in the report-interpretation document, change over a period is a more stable basis than a single day's number.

## Matching the Same Conditions

- Matching the **length (number of days)** of the two periods you want to compare is the basic rule—for example, the 1st–15th of this month versus the 1st–15th of last month.
- If the composition of days of the week differs, the visit pattern can change, so compare periods that include **the same number of each weekday** where possible.
- It is best to mark periods that include public holidays or long holiday breaks separately, and not to compare them mixed in with ordinary periods.

## Using Event Schedule Registration

If you register promotion and event schedules in the dashboard in advance, you can compare the changes in visits and conversion during that period as distinct from normal times. If you want to verify the effect of an event after the fact through period-over-period comparison rather than by "feel," we recommend registering the schedule before the event starts.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I look at month-over-month or year-over-year?
Both are useful. Month-over-month shows the recent trend, while year-over-year shows change that also filters out seasonal factors.

### We opened only recently and have no earlier period to compare against.
Until enough data has accumulated over a sufficient period, check absolute values primarily, and start period-over-period comparison as the data accumulates.

### Can I register an event period later?
Yes. That said, registering it in advance is more useful because you can watch the change together in real time.
