Explains the principles for designing who can access the dashboard and to what extent. For the basic method of obtaining an account and logging in, refer to the Dashboard Access document.

## Only As Much As Needed

As a principle, access is granted **only to the extent needed for the job.** A headquarters manager may need to view multiple stores together, while a store manager often only needs to see their own store. The access scope that fits your organizational structure is designed together at the time of deployment.

## Setting the Access Scope

- **By store** — You can designate the store(s) each account can view.
- **By role** — You can configure the accessible stores or report scope differently according to each team member's job scope.
- The detailed configuration method and the supported scope may vary depending on the contract and product configuration, so we recommend confirming the exact details through your account manager.

## Administrator Responsibilities

- Manage the issuance of accounts for new team members and the deactivation of accounts upon resignation or personnel transfer.
- We recommend periodically checking that no account has been granted broader permissions than necessary.
- If you notice any anomalies related to accounts (such as abnormal login attempts), please notify your account manager.

## Security Recommendations

- We recommend that accounts not be shared among multiple people, but instead be issued and used per individual.
- Set a password that is different from those for other services and hard to guess.
- If you log in from a shared PC, be sure to log out after use.
- If there is a resignation or role change, notify the administrator without delay so that the account can be cleaned up.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can the access scope be set differently for each store?
Yes. You can configure the viewable stores differently for each account. Please request the configuration you need from your account manager.

### Can accounts also be given to external partners (agencies, etc.)?
In many cases this is possible by issuing an account restricted to the necessary scope. Let us know the purpose and the scope needed, and we will guide you.

### Can access logs be checked?
Whether per-account access logs are provided may vary depending on the product configuration. If you need them, please contact your account manager.
