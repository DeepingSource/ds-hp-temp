POS integration is optional. Most reports are available with visit and movement data alone, and you add POS integration when you want to view sales data as well.

## Why Integrate POS

CCTV-based analytics alone can show you visitor counts, dwell time, movement paths, and interest by area. Adding the actual sales data from your POS lets you view reports that combine visits and sales — such as the **share of visitors that led to an actual purchase (purchase conversion rate)**.

## Integration Method

The integration method depends on the POS system your store uses. Typically, the sales data coming from the POS is delivered on a regular basis and matched against the CCTV-based visit data by time and store for comparison.

The specific integration method (data delivery frequency, format, and so on) varies depending on the type of POS system and whether integration is possible, so it is confirmed individually during the pre-inspection.

## Information to Prepare

- **POS system information** — The manufacturer and version of the POS in use, and whether different stores use different systems.
- **Data format** — Information on the form (file, integration program, etc.) in which the sales data can be received.
- **Integration contact** — Contact details for the POS vendor or the in-store POS administrator.

## Verifying the Integration

Once integration is complete, check the purchase conversion rate report in the dashboard to see whether sales data is displayed alongside visitor counts. If the numbers look off, first check the period and filters; if they still differ, contact your account manager.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use the service without POS integration?
Yes. POS integration is optional, and even without it you can use most reports related to visits and movement.

### Can you integrate with any POS system?
We have experience integrating with many POS systems, but whether integration is possible and how it works can vary by system. Let us know which POS you use and we will check for you.

### Is sales data anonymized too?
Sales data is not personally identifiable information; it is handled as aggregate data such as the number of sales and amounts.
