Explains how DeepingSource's anonymization-first approach connects with the personal data regulations of major regions. This document is for reference only; for matters requiring legal judgment, please be sure to have your own legal team review them.

## The Approach

Vision AI ultimately looks at people, and the moment it looks at a person, that information becomes subject to personal data regulations. DeepingSource approaches this problem by erasing identity at the point when CCTV footage is input — the detailed principle is covered in the How Anonymization Works document.

## Regulations by Region

The representative regulations most closely related to the analysis of store CCTV footage are as follows.

- **EU · GDPR** — The European General Data Protection Regulation.
- **US · CCPA** — The California Consumer Privacy Act.
- **KR · PIPA** — Korea's Personal Information Protection Act.

## How Anonymization Reduces Regulatory Risk

Most of the above regulations address how personally identifiable information is collected, retained, and used. Because DeepingSource removes identity at the point of input and does not retain the original footage, it is designed so that personally identifiable information does not accumulate in the first place. However, whether final legal compliance is achieved may vary depending on the region where the store is located, the method of data processing, and the contract terms, so we recommend confirming the specific judgment together with your own legal team.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do separate notification obligations, such as CCTV signage, go away?
No. The obligation to provide notice and disclosure about the operation of CCTV within the store itself may exist separately. Please confirm the obligations under your local laws on your own.

### Does it apply the same way to overseas stores?
The anonymization principle applies the same way, but the detailed procedures and notification obligations required by each region may differ.

### Can we receive Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) materials?
Let your account manager know the type of materials you need, and we will check whether they can be provided.
