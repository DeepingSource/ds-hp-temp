# **What is SOC2 Type 2 Certification?**

Before diving deeper, let's understand the meaning of SOC2 Type 2 certification and why it is crucial for Deeping Source, a company whose core competency lies in data anonymization technology.

SOC2 (Service Organization Control 2) certification is a rigorous auditing framework established by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA). Its purpose is to validate whether service organizations effectively safeguard data to protect their organizations' and customers' privacy.

‍

> The Five Trust Service Principles

SOC2 certification centers around five "trust service principles": security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

Security: Systems are protected against unauthorized physical and logical access.

Availability: Systems are operational and usable as committed or agreed.

Processing Integrity: System processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely, and authorized.

Confidentiality: Confidential information is protected as committed or agreed.

Privacy: Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of in conformity with the organization's privacy notice

‍

> The Difference Between Type 1 and Type 2

It's important to understand the distinction between SOC2 Type 1 and Type 2:

Type 1 reports on the design suitability of a service organization's systems and controls at a specific point in time.

Type 2 builds upon the Type 1 report by additionally covering the operating effectiveness of those controls over a minimum of six months. Thus, Type 2 provides a more comprehensive and reliable assurance that the trust service principles have been adhered to over an extended period.
