How to read the numbers

Absolute numbers aren't a comparison.

Ranking a downtown store against a suburban mall by raw revenue isn't a comparison. Three devices make a real one.

Period comparison

Pick 7, 14, or 30 days and every metric recomputes for that window, measured against the previous window of equal length. “How did the last two weeks do versus the two before?” is the default question.

Peer adjustment

Stores that behave alike are grouped, and each is read by how far it sits from its peer average. Holidays and weather that lift everyone fall away; what's left is the store's own movement.

Source distinction

Dwell is what we measured, revenue is what HQ provided, conversion is an estimate. The screen doesn't show these three with equal weight.
